A house divided

An election carried out during an epidemic and in an atmosphere of deep distrust poses serious risks

Briefing

Sep 3rd 2020

In his final debate with Hillary Clinton in 2016, Donald Trump refused to commit himself to accepting the results of the coming election. The following day he made his position clearer. “I will totally accept the results of this great and historic presidential election,” he said in mock solemnity—before adding, with finger-wagging emphasis: “If I win.” The stubby finger levelled itself at the crowd, which erupted into cheers; the not-yet-president grinned.

  1. debate 争辩
  2. commit 保证
  3. mock 虚假 嘲笑
  4. solemnity 严肃，一本正经
  5. wagging 摇动
  6. emphasis 强调
  7. stubby 短小的
  8. levelled对着
  9. erupted 爆发出
  10. grinned露齿而笑

在2016年与希拉里·克林顿(Hillary Clinton)的最后一场辩论中，唐纳德·特朗普(Donald Trump)拒绝承诺接受即将到来的选举结果。第二天，他把自己的立场说得更清楚了。“我将完全接受这次伟大而具有历史意义的总统选举的结果，”他以一种假装严肃的语气说。然后，他摇着手指强调道:“如果我赢了。”那粗短的手指对准人群，人群中爆发出欢呼声;没有入住白宫咧嘴一笑。

President Trump went on to win with 304 Electoral College votes to Mrs Clinton’s 227, and so how he would in fact have reacted had things gone the other way remains a matter of speculation. This year there appears to be a strong chance that he will not win; The Economist’s election-forecasting model currently puts his chances at one in seven. Mr Trump, though, denies any possibility that he could lose a fair contest: “The only way we’re going to lose this election is if the election is rigged,” he told his followers in August. There can be no real doubt that, should he indeed lose, he would claim that the election was stolen.

  1. Electoral College votes 选举人票
  2. had things gone 虚拟语气
  3. speculation 猜测
  4. chance 可能性
  5. forecasting 预测
  6. rigged 操控
  7. should = if (used when referring to a possible event in the future:)

特朗普赢得了304张选举人票，而希拉里获得了227张选举人票。因此，如果事情朝着相反的方向发展，特朗普实际上会做出怎样的反应，仍然是一个猜测。今年，他很有可能赢不了;根据《经济学人》的选举预测模型，目前他当选的可能性为七分之一。然而，特朗普否认他在公平竞争中失败的任何可能性:“我们输掉这次选举的唯一途径是选举被操纵，”他在8月对他的追随者说。毫无疑问，如果他真的输了，他会声称选举被偷了。

That, come November 4th, such a theft will actually have taken place is remarkably unlikely. Admittedly William Evanina, who directs the National Counter intelligence and Security Centre, says that China and Iran have joined Russia in seeking to influence this election through covert means, presumably emboldened by Russia having paid little price for having done so last time. This is a shocking development. But even if they were all pushing in the same direction—which is unlikely—there is no reason to think that they could decisively tip the result. America’s electoral system is sufficiently decentralised for attempts to rig the vote on a large scale to be incredibly hard. And though voter fraud occasionally takes place, both in-person and by means of absentee ballots, it is harshly punished and very rare; various studies have found the rate to be well below one in a million votes cast.

  1. come +日期： 到某个日期
  2. taken place 发生
  3. Admittedly 不可否认的是
  4. intelligence 情报
  5. seeking 寻求
  6. covert 秘密
  7. means 手段
  8. presumably 大概
  9. 大胆
  10. xxx so 也怎么样
  11. decisively 果断地
  12. tip 改变
  13. sufficiently 相当
  14. decentralised 分散
  15. fraud 欺诈

到11月4日，这样的盗窃案真的发生是不太可能的。不可否认的是，国家反情报与安全中心主任威廉•埃瓦尼纳表示，中国和伊朗已经加入俄罗斯的行列，试图通过秘密手段影响这次选举，大概是因为俄罗斯上次这么做并没有付出什么代价。这是一个令人震惊的发展。但是，即使他们都朝着同一个方向努力——这是不可能的——也没有理由认为他们可以决定性地改变结果。美国的选举系统是相当分散的，因此想要大规模地操纵选举是非常困难的。虽然选举舞弊偶尔会发生，无论是当面投票还是缺席投票，但这种情况会受到严厉惩罚，而且非常罕见;各种研究发现，这一比率远低于百万分之一。

But this does not mean that Mr Trump’s protestations will be of no account. America is deeply polarised, and in a few places armed partisans have taken to the streets. Both parties have portrayed this year’s contest as existentially important to America’s future, warning that the country will be forever altered for the worse if the other candidate wins. The new round of foreign interference, like the lies and fearmongering from the president himself, add both to the stock of disinformation and the pervasive sense that things are not to be trusted. As a result a significant number of Americans of all political stripes doubt that the election will be held fairly (see chart). On top of it all, the election is being held during an epidemic that will, by election day, have killed over 200,000.

  1. protestations 声明
  2. be of no account 无足轻重
  3. polarised 两极分化
  4. taken to the streets 上街游行
  5. partisans 政党支持者。
  6. portrayed 描绘
  7. existentially 存在地
  8. interference 干涉
  9. fearmongering 谎言
  10. stock 存货
  11. pervasive 无处不在的
  12. significant 相当多的
  13. all political stripes 所有的政治条纹
  14. On top of it all 最重要的是
  15. epidemic 疫情

但这并不意味着特朗普的声明毫无意义。美国两极分化严重，在一些地方武装分子甚至走上街头。两党都表示，今年的竞选对美国的未来至关重要，并警告说，如果另一位候选人获胜，美国将永远变得更糟。新一轮的外国干涉，就像总统自己的谎言和制造恐慌一样，增加了虚假信息的数量，也增加了人们普遍认为事情不值得信任的感觉。其结果是，相当多的美国人对选举是否公平表示怀疑(见图表)。最重要的是，选举是在一场流行病期间举行的，到选举日，这场流行病将导致20多万人死亡。

In June a bipartisan group of campaign veterans, elected officials, journalists and academics convened by the Transition Integrity Project, a group founded last year, set about war-gaming four different possible election results: a commanding victory for Joe Biden, a narrow victory for Mr Biden, a narrow victory for Mr Trump achieved, as his previous one was, without a majority of the popular vote, and a result in which, because of contested outcomes in battleground states, the identity of the victor was unclear. In all four scenarios the role playing produced levels of gamesmanship and tumult beyond anything seen in recent American elections. In the narrow-Biden- victory scenario the Secret Service escorted Mr Trump from the White House on inauguration day. It is hard to overestimate what such a sight would mean to Americans—and to the rest of the world.

  1. bipartisan 两党
  2. veterans 老手
  3. campaign 竞选
  4. journalists 记者
  5. convened 召开
  6. war-gaming 沙盘模拟
  7. commanding 威风凛凛的
  8. commanding victory 大胜
  9. narrow victory 小胜
  10. achieved 实现
  11. contested outcomes 有争议的结果
  12. scenarios 场景
  13. gamesmanship 战术
  14. tumult 骚乱
  15. Secret
  16. inauguration 就职典礼
  17. It is hard to overestimate 怎么夸张都不为过

6月，一个由竞选老手、当选官员、记者和学者组成的两党团体，由去年成立的过渡诚信项目(Transition Integrity Project)召集，开始对四种可能的选举结果进行战争模拟:拜登(Joe Biden)获得压倒性胜利，拜登险胜，特朗普获得了险胜，就像他上次的胜利一样，没有获得多数选民的支持，而且由于战场州的选举结果存在争议，胜利者的身份尚不清楚。在这四种情况下，角色扮演所产生的花招和混乱程度超过了最近的美国大选。在拜登险胜的情况下，特勤局在特朗普就职典礼当天护送他离开白宫。很难高估这样的景象对美国人以及世界其他地区的意义。
